About Me

I'm an Economics and Mathematics student at IBA Karachi, and most of what genuinely excites me sits at the boundary between AI and how humans — and AI agents — make decisions together. My final-year project, ELICIT (Emergent LLM Institutions for Climate and International Treaties), is where that interest has taken its most concrete form: a multi-agent simulation framework where LLM-driven agents must negotiate cooperation, impose sanctions, share climate risk, and even vote to rewrite their own governing rules — all across repeated public goods interactions modelled on real international climate treaty dynamics.

The core question ELICIT investigates is whether LLM agents can form and sustain institutions. Each agent chooses which institution to join, how much to contribute to a shared pool, whether to punish or reward peers, and participates in constitutional democracy sessions that reshape the rules mid-simulation. The framework layers in climate shocks, a Loss & Damage Fund that mirrors real UN mechanisms, Theory of Mind reasoning, and reputation-driven gossip — turning it into a testbed for studying emergent governance under uncertainty.

Beyond ELICIT, I care about building ML systems that actually ship: production MLOps pipelines, hybrid retrieval systems for domain-specific knowledge, and forecasting models grounded in real economic data. I'm drawn to problems where rigorous quantitative thinking and AI intersect — whether that's modelling cooperation in climate negotiations, detecting hallucinated LLM outputs, or forecasting inflation with ensemble methods. I'm looking for research roles and opportunities where I can push further into agent-based AI, LLM reasoning, and applied machine learning.
